# State/Latent Abstraction on Top of TD-MPC2: Will an Abstraction Module Actually Help?

## TL;DR
- **The single most defensible bet is a behavioral/bisimulation-style abstraction objective on the latent (family B), but only if you target the failure modes TD-MPC2 does NOT already cover — high-dimensional locomotion (Dog/Humanoid) stability and distractor/transfer robustness — NOT clean single-task exploration like HopperHop.** TD-MPC2's existing latent-consistency loss already implements self-predictive representations (family D), so family D is largely redundant; families A and C are unlikely to give a clean architecture-level win on TD-MPC2's home turf.
- **Direct evidence already exists that abstraction on TD-MPC/TD-MPC2 can help:** BS-MPC (bisimulation added to TD-MPC, arXiv 2410.04553) beats TD-MPC on clean high-dim state tasks and decisively under distractors while training ~15–20% faster; Discrete Codebook World Models / DC-MPC (arXiv 2503.00653) shows a discrete latent space modestly beats TD-MPC2 in compute-matched ablations and "significantly outperforms TD-MPC2 and DreamerV3" on high-dimensional Dog Run/Humanoid Walk. Both wins are concentrated on high-dimensional locomotion and noisy observations — not exploration-bound tasks.
- **The cleanest, reviewer-proof experiment is a compute-matched sample-efficiency comparison on the canonical hard DMControl Dog/Humanoid subset plus a distractor or multi-task generalization split, reported with rliable IQM + 95% CIs over ≥5 seeds (Agarwal et al. 2021, arXiv 2108.13264), with the abstraction module as the ONLY changed variable.** Drop HopperHop; it measures basin-entry luck, not representation quality.

## Key Findings

1. **TD-MPC2 (arXiv 2310.16828) already implements self-predictive representation learning.** Its latent "consistency"/temporal-consistency loss trains the encoder so the latent dynamics predicts the next encoded latent — mechanically the same idea as SPR (arXiv 2007.05929) and the self-predictive abstraction unified by Ni et al. (arXiv 2401.08898), which explicitly groups TD-MPC with DeepMDP/DBC/SPR/MICo under one "self-predictive" condition. Adding another self-predictive auxiliary loss (family D) is therefore mostly redundant. The user should not reinvent this.
2. **TD-MPC2's SimNorm already provides much of what a naive "clustering/sparsification" abstraction would provide.** SimNorm projects the latent onto L fixed-dimensional simplices via softmax, biasing toward sparse, quasi-discrete codes and preventing latent explosion — the TD-MPC2 paper states SimNorm "is essential to the training stability of TD-MPC2." A prototype-clustering module risks duplicating SimNorm's sparsity/regularization effect rather than adding new structure.
3. **Bisimulation/behavioral metrics (family B) add a genuinely different signal: reward-and-dynamics-grounded invariance.** Whereas TD-MPC2's consistency loss only requires the latent to be self-predictable, a bisimulation loss requires latents to be CLOSE when they have similar immediate reward and similar next-state distributions — explicitly collapsing behaviorally-equivalent states and discarding task-irrelevant variation. This is the one mechanism with a clear gap relative to TD-MPC2.
4. **The gains from bisimulation are strongest exactly where TD-MPC2 is weakest or where reconstruction-based methods fail: distractors, high-dimensional locomotion, and transfer.** On clean low-dimensional dense-reward tasks, bisimulation typically does NOT beat strong baselines and can be unstable (embedding collapse/explosion).
5. **Temporal abstraction/hierarchy (family C) addresses long-horizon, sparse-reward credit assignment** — but TD-MPC2's short MPPI horizon is already backed by a bootstrapped TD value function, so the marginal benefit on standard DMControl/Meta-World (mostly dense, ≤1000 steps) is small. Hierarchy is the right tool only if you move to genuinely long-horizon sparse-reward tasks.

## Details by Abstraction Family

### Family A — MDP Homomorphisms / State Aggregation / Model Minimization

**Methods:** Ravindran & Barto MDP homomorphisms; Givan/Dean/Greig model minimization (exact & approximate stochastic bisimulation/state aggregation); modern deep MDP Homomorphic Networks (van der Pol et al., arXiv 2006.16908) and Plannable Approximations to MDP Homomorphisms (AAMAS 2020); equivariant/symmetry-based abstraction.

1. *Mechanism.* Classical: partition the state(-action) space into equivalence classes that preserve reward and transition structure ("model minimization"), then solve the smaller quotient MDP. Deep MDP Homomorphic Networks build group symmetries (reflections/rotations) of the joint state-action space directly into policy/value networks via equivariance constraints, constructed numerically with a "symmetrizer." Plannable Approximations learn the homomorphism with a contrastive, action-equivariant loss.
2. *Clean compute-matched win.* MDP Homomorphic Networks show faster convergence than unstructured baselines on CartPole, a grid world, and Pong — small, symmetric domains. There is **no demonstrated clean win over Dreamer/TD-MPC2/MuZero/EfficientZero** on a standard continuous-control suite. This is a single-lineage result on toy/symmetric tasks, not independently replicated against strong MBRL baselines.
3. *Intuition.* Fixes sample efficiency by weight-sharing across symmetric state-action pairs (re-using experience from "mirror-image" states).
4. *Fit to TD-MPC2.* Weak. Requires known/identifiable symmetry groups; most DMControl/Meta-World/MyoSuite tasks lack clean exploitable global symmetries, and TD-MPC2's proprioceptive inputs aren't naturally equivariant. High implementation cost, narrow applicability.
5. *Negative results / failure modes.* Benefits evaporate when symmetries are absent, approximate, or mis-specified; exact model minimization is intractable at scale (the original motivation for DeepMDP). Not a credible architecture-level add-on for TD-MPC2.

### Family B — Bisimulation / Behavioral State Abstraction  ← most promising

**Methods:** DeepMDP (Gelada et al., arXiv 1906.02736); Deep Bisimulation for Control / DBC (Zhang et al., arXiv 2006.10742); MICo (Castro et al., arXiv 2106.08229); robust bisimulation (Kemertas & Aumentado-Armstrong, arXiv 2110.14096); SimSR; revised bisimulation metric (arXiv 2507.18519); and TD-MPC-specific BS-MPC (arXiv 2410.04553).

1. *Mechanism.*
   - **DeepMDP:** trains a latent model with two losses — reward prediction and next-latent-distribution prediction — and proves that with a Wasserstein latent transition loss this upper-bounds the bisimulation distance. Notably, DeepMDP's two losses are essentially what TD-MPC2 already does (reward + latent-dynamics prediction).
   - **DBC:** trains the encoder so that ℓ1 distance in latent space equals the π\*-bisimulation metric; uses a Gaussian-transition 2-Wasserstein closed form. Reconstruction-free; explicitly discards task-irrelevant info.
   - **MICo:** a sampling-based behavioral distance (Matching under Independent Couplings) learned as an auxiliary loss alongside the value function, with no extra network parameters; uses independent couplings to avoid the cost of the Kantorovich/Wasserstein optimization. Strong results on Arcade Learning Environment and DMControl.
   - **BS-MPC:** adds a 4th loss term to TD-MPC's reward/value/consistency objective — a π\*-bisimulation encoder loss of the form (‖h(s)−h(ṡ)‖₁ − |r−ṙ| − γ‖z̄′−z̄̇′‖²)² over permuted batch pairs (the on-policy bisimulation metric of Zhang et al. 2021, which "measure[s] behavioral equivalence between states by comparing their immediate rewards and next state distributions"). It also encodes every observation in the rollout (not just the first), giving the encoder a direct gradient and enabling parallelism. Same architecture/params/hyperparameters as TD-MPC except the c₄ term.
2. *Clean compute-matched win.*
   - **DBC:** on Distracting/natural-video-background MuJoCo and a highway-driving task, DBC substantially outperforms reconstruction-based (SAC+AE), contrastive, DeepMDP, and SLAC baselines; on clean tasks it is only competitive with SAC.
   - **MICo:** improves over the baseline agent on both Atari (ALE) and DMControl across 5 seeds (the paper reports a "clear advantage" in aggregate; gains are auxiliary-loss-level, not a new SOTA agent).
   - **BS-MPC (most directly relevant):** identical-architecture comparison to TD-MPC. On clean high-dimensional STATE tasks it "significantly outperforms TD-MPC, SAC, and Dreamer-v3" on Dog/Humanoid and avoids a TD-MPC training collapse that appears after ~4M steps. On clean IMAGE tasks (10 DMControl, 3M steps) it is only "competitive with TD-MPC, DrQ-v2 and Dreamer-v3." On distractor (Kinetics-background) image tasks it "constantly outperforms TD-MPC in every environment." It is also **~15–20% FASTER** to train than TD-MPC (parallelizable encoder; e.g., Walker-run 8.3h vs 10.1h on an RTX-4090). Crucially, **vs TD-MPC2 it is only "comparable or slightly better," and TD-MPC2 converges faster on some tasks (quadruped-run, walker-run).** Reliability caveats: 3 seeds for main tasks (5 for distractors); SAC/Dreamer-v3 numbers taken from the TD-MPC2 paper rather than re-run; per-environment tuning of c₄ over a grid {1e-8 … 0.5} is required.
3. *Intuition.* Fixes distractor robustness (collapses behaviorally-equivalent observations, ignoring task-irrelevant background), and — per BS-MPC — fixes a latent-stability/representation-collapse failure that TD-MPC's pure consistency loss can suffer in high-dimensional locomotion (where its consistency loss "gradually increases and diverges"). Also linked to causal feature sets → better generalization/transfer.
4. *Fit to TD-MPC2.* **This is the family with a real gap.** TD-MPC2's consistency loss makes the latent self-predictable but does NOT force behaviorally-equivalent states together or task-irrelevant variation out. A bisimulation/behavioral-metric auxiliary loss adds reward-and-dynamics-grounded invariance. BS-MPC is essentially a proof of concept that this works on the TD-MPC skeleton. BUT: TD-MPC2 is reconstruction-free already, so it may inherit some distractor robustness; the open question is whether bisimulation gains survive on CLEAN proprioceptive benchmarks (BS-MPC suggests yes on Dog/Humanoid via stability, marginal elsewhere).
5. *Negative results / failure modes.* Bisimulation metric learning is prone to embedding collapse (φ→constant) and norm explosion, especially under sparse rewards (Kemertas & Aumentado-Armstrong, arXiv 2110.14096, document this and require norm constraints + intrinsic rewards to fix). The metric is "pessimistic" (worst-case), can mis-measure distinct states, and recent work (arXiv 2507.18519) revises the reward-gap definition. On clean dense-reward low-dim tasks bisimulation typically does not beat strong baselines. Per-task coefficient tuning (BS-MPC's c₄) is a real reproducibility burden.

### Family C — Temporal Abstraction / Hierarchy / Skills

**Methods:** Options framework (Sutton, Precup & Singh 1999, semi-MDP); Option-Critic; Director (Hafner et al., arXiv 2206.04114); HIRO; HAC; DIAYN; LEXA; jumpy/hierarchical world models (e.g. HIEROS, arXiv 2310.05167).

1. *Mechanism.* Options = temporally-extended actions (initiation set, internal policy, termination). Director learns a goal autoencoder that compresses world-model features into discrete codes; a manager policy "selects abstract actions in this discrete space every K = 8 steps, which the goal decoder then turns into feature space goals," and a worker policy achieves them. HIEROS stacks a hierarchy of S5-based world models. DIAYN learns skills by maximizing mutual information between skills and visited states.
2. *Clean compute-matched win.* Director solves long-horizon sparse-reward tasks (e.g., visual pin-pad, ant-maze, Atari) where flat agents fail, and is competitive with flat baselines on standard tasks. HIEROS claims SOTA mean/median human-normalized score on a 25-game Atari100k subset over DreamerV3/IRIS/TWM. **Critically, there is NO demonstration that temporal abstraction beats TD-MPC2 on standard DMControl proprioceptive control;** hierarchy's wins are on long-horizon sparse-reward regimes that TD-MPC2 isn't primarily designed for. Single-paper claims, weaker or different baselines.
3. *Intuition.* Fixes long-horizon credit assignment and exploration under sparse rewards by acting/exploring at a coarser temporal scale.
4. *Fit to TD-MPC2.* Limited on TD-MPC2's home benchmarks. TD-MPC2's short ~3-step MPPI horizon is bootstrapped by a TD-learned terminal value function, which already provides long-horizon return estimates; most DMControl/Meta-World tasks are dense-reward and ≤1000 steps. Hierarchy adds value only if you move to genuinely long-horizon, sparse-reward, or multi-stage tasks. There IS an IQL-TD-MPC hierarchical manager (Chitnis et al.) for offline sparse-reward, suggesting a niche.
5. *Negative results / failure modes.* HRL is notoriously hard to train (goal non-stationarity, collapse of skills/options to degenerate solutions, manager-worker instability). On dense-reward control the overhead usually doesn't pay off. DIAYN skills on DMControl Hopper degenerate to static postures (per the Hopper benchmark critique, arXiv 2410.08870).

### Family D — Self-Predictive / Contrastive Representations  ← largely redundant with TD-MPC2

**Methods:** SPR (Schwarzer et al., arXiv 2007.05929); BYOL-Explore (Guo et al., arXiv 2206.08332); self-predictive theory (Tang et al., arXiv 2212.03319; Ni et al., arXiv 2401.08898).

1. *Mechanism.* SPR predicts its own future latent representations multiple steps ahead using a learned transition model + EMA target encoder + projection, plus data-augmentation consistency. BYOL-Explore optimizes a single latent-prediction loss and uses its prediction error as an intrinsic exploration reward. Ni et al. show DeepMDP/DBC/SPR/MICo/TD-MPC all reduce to a common "self-predictive" abstraction condition; stop-gradient prevents collapse.
2. *Clean compute-matched win.* SPR: "achieves a median human-normalized score of 0.415 on Atari in a setting limited to 100k steps... a 55% relative improvement over the previous state-of-the-art," and "outperforms human expert scores on 7 out of 26 games." BYOL-Explore: superhuman on the 10 hardest-exploration Atari games and solves most DM-HARD-8 tasks from extrinsic+intrinsic reward where prior methods needed demonstrations. These are strong, but the comparison baselines are model-free/older; the SPR mechanism is now standard.
3. *Intuition.* Fixes sample efficiency (denser learning signal from structure-in-dynamics) and, for BYOL-Explore, exploration (curiosity from prediction error).
4. *Fit to TD-MPC2.* **Redundant.** TD-MPC2's latent-consistency loss IS a self-predictive objective (Ni et al. and the TD-MPC papers explicitly group it with SPR/DeepMDP). The original TD-MPC ablation showed its latent-consistency loss already beats both reconstruction and contrastive auxiliary losses on 15 DMControl tasks. Adding SPR-style multi-step prediction or a BYOL loss would mostly duplicate existing machinery. The only non-redundant slice is BYOL-Explore-style INTRINSIC REWARD for exploration — but that's an exploration trick, not abstraction, and the user explicitly wants to avoid exploration-luck testbeds.
5. *Negative results / failure modes.* Self-predictive losses can collapse to trivial constant representations without careful optimization (faster predictor + stop-gradient/semi-gradient, per Tang et al. and Ni et al.). The benefit over a well-tuned latent-consistency loss (which TD-MPC2 has) is marginal.

## Decision Questions

### Q1 — Which single abstraction mechanism is most likely to give a real, architecture-level gain over TD-MPC2?

**A behavioral/bisimulation-style abstraction loss on the latent (family B).** Reasoning: (i) It is the only family that adds a signal TD-MPC2 genuinely lacks — reward-and-dynamics-grounded behavioral invariance, as opposed to mere self-predictability (which TD-MPC2 already has) and sparsity (which SimNorm already provides). (ii) There is direct, TD-MPC-specific evidence it works: BS-MPC beats TD-MPC on clean high-dim state tasks and decisively under distractors, while being faster to train; and DC-MPC's discrete-latent ablation modestly beats TD-MPC2 in a compute-matched test, "significantly outperform[ing] TD-MPC2 and DreamerV3" on Dog Run/Humanoid Walk. (iii) The user's prototype-clustering "Glass" module can be reframed as a behavioral-abstraction objective: instead of clustering by latent similarity (redundant with SimNorm), cluster/aggregate by behavioral equivalence (reward + next-latent distribution), which is what makes the abstraction informative. Structural-entropy on a behavior-grounded graph is a defensible novelty IF it demonstrably collapses behaviorally-equivalent states better than a plain bisimulation loss. **The honest caveat:** against TD-MPC2 specifically (not TD-MPC), even BS-MPC is only "comparable or slightly better," so the bar is high and the win will likely be concentrated in high-dim locomotion, distractor robustness, and transfer rather than across-the-board.

### Q2 — Standard fair evaluation protocol, and which benchmark isolates abstraction value?

**Protocol (the field standard, set by Agarwal et al. 2021, arXiv 2108.13264):** report **rliable interquartile mean (IQM) and median with 95% stratified-bootstrap confidence intervals**, plus **performance profiles** and **optimality gap**, over **≥5 seeds** (IQM is reliable with as few as ~5–10 runs; point estimates of mean/median are not). Show **sample-efficiency curves** (normalized score vs environment steps) at fixed compute/step budgets, not just asymptotic "best reward reached" (the metric the user correctly abandoned).

**Benchmarks used in this subfield:**
- **DMControl proprioceptive** (TD-MPC2's home; the canonical hard subset is Dog Run/Walk/Stand/Trot, Humanoid Stand/Walk/Run, Acrobot Swingup, plus Walker/Quadruped/Reacher Hard; DC-MPC's DMControl-10 — Acrobot Swingup, Dog Run/Walk/Stand/Trot, Humanoid Stand/Walk/Run, Reacher Hard, Walker Walk — is a good template).
- **DMControl pixels + Distracting Control Suite / DMC-VB (arXiv 2101.02722, arXiv 2409.18330)** — natural-video / camera / color distractors.
- **Atari100k** (data-efficiency, discrete).
- **Meta-World** (multi-task / manipulation).
- **Crafter** (long-horizon, open-ended).

**Which benchmark best ISOLATES abstraction value (not exploration luck or raw capacity)?** Two complementary choices:
1. **Distractor benchmarks (Distracting Control Suite / DMC-VB)** isolate the representation's ability to discard task-irrelevant variation — the cleanest test of *behavioral* abstraction, because raw capacity and exploration are held roughly constant while only nuisance variation changes. This is where bisimulation has its most replicated wins.
2. **High-dimensional clean locomotion (Dog/Humanoid) under a fixed sample budget** isolates representation/optimization quality and sample efficiency — where both BS-MPC and DC-MPC show their TD-MPC(2) wins.

**Avoid** single-task exploration-bound tasks like **HopperHop** (the user's bad testbed) and other bimodal basin-entry tasks; the Hopper-benchmark critique (arXiv 2410.08870) documents that DMC Hopper outcomes are dominated by exploration luck and that even strong model-based methods get ~0 reward on the DMC Hopper variant, so it confounds abstraction quality with luck.

### Q3 — Cleanest minimal experiment a skeptical reviewer would believe

**Single-variable, compute-matched, multi-seed, on benchmarks chosen to isolate abstraction:**

1. **Baseline & ablation parity.** Take the official TD-MPC2 codebase. The ONLY change is the addition of the abstraction module ("Glass"). Match parameter count, gradient steps, wall-clock/compute, planning horizon, and all hyperparameters. Report the added compute overhead explicitly. (BS-MPC's "the only difference is the c₄ encoder loss" design is the gold standard to emulate.)
2. **Primary axis — sample efficiency on the hard clean subset.** DMControl Dog Run, Dog Trot, Humanoid Walk, Humanoid Run, Acrobot Swingup, Quadruped Run, plus 4–6 easier tasks for breadth. Report normalized-score IQM + 95% CI at fixed budgets (e.g., 500k and 1M steps), with sample-efficiency curves. **≥5 seeds.** Expected, defensible win: faster convergence and/or higher IQM on the high-dim locomotion tasks, mirroring BS-MPC/DC-MPC.
3. **Second axis — the abstraction-isolating test.** Add a distractor split (Distracting Control Suite, natural-video backgrounds) AND/OR a multi-task/transfer split. For transfer, exploit TD-MPC2's multi-task setting: TD-MPC2 trains "a single 317M parameter agent to perform 80 tasks across multiple task domains, embodiments, and action spaces" (released models span 30/70/80 tasks). Train multi-task TD-MPC2 with vs without the abstraction module, and measure (a) multi-task IQM and (b) few-shot finetuning/transfer to held-out tasks. Abstraction should help most where representations must be shared across tasks/embodiments.
4. **Mechanism evidence, not just scores.** Show the abstraction does what you claim: e.g., latent-space distance correlates with behavioral (reward+dynamics) distance; behaviorally-equivalent states are collapsed; under distractors the latent ignores nuisance variation (probe accuracy on task vs distractor factors, as in DMC-VB). This converts a "number went up" claim into an "architecture-level mechanism" claim.
5. **Pre-register the kill criterion.** If, under compute-matching and ≥5 seeds, the abstraction does not improve IQM with non-overlapping or clearly-separated 95% CIs on EITHER the hard-locomotion sample-efficiency axis OR the distractor/transfer axis, the mechanism does not clear the bar — and no training-procedure trick should be used to rescue it.

**Benchmarks that would CHANGE the recommendation:** If the goal shifts to long-horizon sparse-reward (Crafter, ant-maze, multi-stage manipulation), family C (temporal abstraction/Director-style hierarchy) becomes the better bet. If the goal is pure pixel-based distractor robustness, weight family B even more heavily. If results show the gain is purely from extra effective capacity/regularization, re-run with a capacity-matched vanilla TD-MPC2 (wider MLPs) to rule out the trivial explanation.

## Recommendations

1. **Reframe "Glass" from latent-similarity clustering to behavioral-equivalence abstraction (family B).** Latent-similarity clustering risks duplicating SimNorm's sparsification and the consistency loss's self-predictivity. Ground the clustering/structural-entropy objective in reward + next-latent-distribution similarity (bisimulation-flavored), which is the one signal TD-MPC2 lacks. Position the structural-entropy auxiliary as a hierarchical/soft alternative to the pairwise bisimulation loss — but you must show it beats a plain bisimulation auxiliary (BS-MPC-style) to claim novelty.
2. **Drop HopperHop and "best reward reached."** Use rliable IQM + 95% CIs over ≥5 seeds on sample-efficiency curves.
3. **Run the Q3 experiment**: compute-matched, single-variable, on hard clean DMControl locomotion (Dog/Humanoid) for sample efficiency, PLUS a distractor split and/or the multi-task/transfer split, with mechanism probes.
4. **Set staged go/no-go gates.** Stage 1: does the module beat vanilla TD-MPC2 IQM on hard-locomotion sample efficiency at 1M steps (≥5 seeds, separated CIs)? Stage 2: does it help under distractors? Stage 3: does it help multi-task/transfer? Proceed only as long as at least one axis shows a clean win; abandon if none do.
5. **Explicitly benchmark against BS-MPC and DC-MPC**, since they are the prior art for "abstraction on TD-MPC(2)." Your method must be compared to a plain bisimulation auxiliary (BS-MPC-style) and to a discrete-codebook latent (DC-MPC-style) to demonstrate the structural-entropy clustering adds something beyond them.

## Caveats
- **Both TD-MPC-specific abstraction papers (BS-MPC, DC-MPC) report results primarily as training curves, not numeric tables, use only 3 seeds for key baselines, and import some baseline numbers from the TD-MPC2 paper/repo rather than re-running them.** Treat their head-to-head margins as indicative, not definitive. Against TD-MPC2 specifically (not the older TD-MPC), both are only "comparable or modestly better" overall, with wins concentrated on high-dim locomotion. DC-MPC also confounds latent design with other changes (TD3 prior, decoupled value learning, REDQ-style critic ensemble), so its cleanest evidence is the controlled "DCWM-inside-TD-MPC2" ablation, which showed only "some improvement" on DMControl-10 and roughly equal performance on Meta-World-10.
- **Bisimulation methods have documented instability** (embedding collapse/explosion under sparse reward) and need norm constraints/intrinsic rewards; budget for this engineering.
- **Family D is largely redundant with TD-MPC2's existing consistency loss** — do not spend effort there except possibly BYOL-Explore-style intrinsic reward, which is an exploration mechanism, not abstraction.
- **Family A (homomorphic/equivariant) and Family C (hierarchy)** lack any demonstrated clean win over TD-MPC2 on its standard benchmarks; pursue them only if the problem definition changes (known symmetries; long-horizon sparse reward).
- **DC-MPC additionally found that adding observation reconstruction "harms performance" and can "prevent learning completely" on Dog Run/Humanoid Walk**, and that replacing DreamerV3's latent with a codebook "does not improve performance" — i.e., latent-design gains are architecture-specific and do not transfer freely across world-model families. Expect your abstraction module's effect to be similarly TD-MPC2-specific and to require validation, not assumption.
- **Statistical-significance risk:** with high-variance DMControl tasks, even a real improvement may need ≥5–10 seeds to show separated CIs; under-powered comparisons are the most common reason abstraction claims fail review.
- Numbers cited (SPR 0.415 median Atari100k; BS-MPC training-time table and ~15–20% speedup; TD-MPC2's 104 tasks / 317M-param 80-task model) are from the primary papers; where a source reported only figures rather than tables, this report says so rather than inventing point estimates.
